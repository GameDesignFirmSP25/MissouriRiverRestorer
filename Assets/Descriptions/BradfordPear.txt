Bradford Pear, “Pyrus calleryana”
  
Physical Description: An ornamental tree that can grow between 30-50 feet tall. It produces small, inedible, greenish-yellow fruits. They have vibrant, glossy green foliage in the spring. In the fall, the leaves turn a reddish-purple to bronze-red. That being said, these trees have especially weak limbs and are prone to frequent breakage.
  
Ecological Function: Invasive species, but does attract birds. Known for its distinct smell, this tree also attracts blowflies–which is their main pollinator.
  
Blooms in spring (around April). 
  
Native to China and Taiwan.
