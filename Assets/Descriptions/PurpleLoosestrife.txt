Purple Loosestrife, "Lythrum Salicaria"
  
Physical Description: A tall, multi-stemmed perennial herb with purple flowers that bloom in long, terminal spikes. Can grow to be anywhere from 2-10 feet tall, 5 feet being the average.
  
Ecological Function:  Invasive; positive impacts include water purification, wildlife habitation, and ecosystem restoration.
  
Blooms from July to September.
  
Higher concentrations in northern Missouri, but can be found anywhere. Marshes, wet prairies, along streams, around farm ponds, and in moist fields, pastures, and roadside ditches are specific areas of propagation.
