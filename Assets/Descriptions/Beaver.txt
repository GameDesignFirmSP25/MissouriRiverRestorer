Beaver, “Castor canadensis”
  
Physical Description: Large rodent with webbed hind feet. Relatively hairless, horizontally flattened tail. It has a blunt head with small eyes and ears, a short neck, and a stout body.
  
Ecological Function: Known as “ecosystem engineers,” creating habitats that other species are dependent on. They mainly eat woody and nonwoody vegetation, depending on the season.
  
Beavers live in colonies, consisting of a male and female as well as their kits. The young are weaned after about 6 weeks but remain with the family for about 2 years.
  
Commonly found in and along streams, rivers, marshes, and small lakes.
