Northern Map turtle, "Graptemys geographica"
  
Physical description: Also known as the common map turtle. Medium sized turtles with a brown shell and yellow lines that are akin to a map (hence the name), limbs are a dark brown with yellowish lines. Has a yellow mark above the eye.
  
Ecological Function: Keeps smaller creature populations in check. Males eat snails, small mussels, and aquatic insects. Females eat crayfish, snails, insect larvae, and underwater molluscs, including the invasive zebra mussel. Their eggs and young are food to raccoons, skunks, foxes, birds, otters, and coyotes.
  
Active from March-October, otherwise primarily huddles together at the bottom of lakes/rivers
  
Near primarily rivers everywhere except the northwest and southeast of Missouri
