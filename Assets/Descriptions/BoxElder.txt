Box Elder, "Acer Negundo"
  
Physical description: 40-70 ft tree with narrow-ish leaves that are in sets of 3-5, has slender and shiny twigs that have a white coating, the twigs themselves being purplish-brown or green. Has green, drooping flowers and winged seeds that twirl akin to a helicopter when they fall.
  
Ecological Function: A quick growing and weak tree that can easily be knocked over, as well as attracting bugs that can be a nuisance. The seeds however are plentiful and feed many bugs, squirrels, and other small mammals.
  
Flowers from April-May and grows seeds August-October that can last into the winter.
  
Lives statewide in most areas, from near homes to the edges of swamps and forests.
