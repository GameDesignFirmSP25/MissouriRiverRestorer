Muskrat, "Ondatra zibethicus"
  
Physical description: A medium and brown mammal covered in fur that has stubby front legs and powerful hind legs with large, webbed feet. Has a scaly and tapered tail.
  
Ecological Function: Deemed a nuisance due to their burrowing destroying dams, hunting has been authorized during certain times and under certain circumstances. Eats various plant stems including corn and cattails, also eats clams, fish, frogs, and other aquatic plants.
  
Breeding occurs from late winter to mid-September, primarily at the end of March, April, and May. Produces 2-3 litters per year, most ready to breed by the next spring after theyâ€™re born.
  
Semi-aquatic, living near marshes, rivers, and streams throughout all of Missouri.
