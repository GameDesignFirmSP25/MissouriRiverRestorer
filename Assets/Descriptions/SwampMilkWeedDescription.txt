Swamp milkweed, “Asclepias incarnata”
  
Physical Description: Can grow up to four feet tall, with a range of white, pink, and purple flowers that typically bloom in the summer. These flowers appear in tight clusters at the stems due to the branching nature of the plant. When stems are cut, they exude a toxic milky sap.
  
Ecological Function: Food source for adult butterflies, hummingbirds, and monarch butterflies.
Blooms in summer (July-August)
  
Commonly found in swamps, river bottomlands, and wet meadows.
