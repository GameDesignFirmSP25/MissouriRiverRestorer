European Starling, “Sturnus vulgaris”
  
Physical Description: Stocky, short-tailed invasive birds with a distinctive chunky shape. Feathers are a glossy greenish black. Has a long, sharp-pointed yellow bill. Plumage is speckled with white spots.
  
Ecological Function: A widespread invasive species that primarily feast on insects. They are extremely common today, posing potential threats to local ecosystems and agriculture, and just generally regarded as “pests.” They compete with native species for nesting space.
  
Young fledge in 21-23 days, and there are 1-2 broods per year.
  
Native to Eurasia and northern Africa.
