Sycamore, "Platanus Occidentalis"
  
Physical description: Large tree with a large trunk and spreading and crooked white branches. Has 4-8 inch green leaves with three main points. Bark is smooth and green while young, ending up a reddish-brown. Has red/yellow flowers and brown, circular clusters of seeds.
  
Ecological Function: It provides food during the winter for birds and many animals live inside of its trunk.
  
Flowers from April-June and grows seeds September-October that last during the winter.
  
Lives statewide on gravel bars or near wet areas, often planted throughout urban environments.
