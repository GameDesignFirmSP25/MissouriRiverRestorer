Cordgrass, "Spartina pectinata"
  
Physical description: A seven foot tall grass with a green stalk, coarse leaf blades, and 7-30 clusters of seed heads near the top that are brown.
  
Ecological Function: Dense colonies prevent soil corrosion, also eaten by many insects, waterfowl, and grazing mammals.
  
Rarely produces seeds, when it does the seeds will just fall away.
  
Lives scattered about statewide in prairies and marshlands, as well as near areas where seeds scatter when harvested and transported for production into hay.
