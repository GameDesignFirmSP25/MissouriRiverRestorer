Painted Lady Butterfly, "Vanessa Cardui"
  
Physical description: Larva are greenish-yellowish with yellow spines and stripes on the sides. Adults are orange and black, with more orange closer to the body and more black further out. Has white spots that line the wingâ€™s edges and on the tips of the wings. Has four small white spots on the underside of the wings, as opposed to the American lady which has two large ones.
  
Ecological Function: Helps pollinate and feeds on over 100+ plants, especially thistle (which it helps keep in check), hollyhock, and mallow. All stages of life provide food for predators.
  
Migrates through Missouri from March-November, lays eggs frequently even if the location is not optimal to just lay as many as possible
  
Most widespread butterfly worldwide, lives throughout all of Missouri
